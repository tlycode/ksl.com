Uproar over Creepy Antique Dolls in Provo

By Jordan Ormond, KSL.com | Updated - Feb 10th, 2019 @ 12:37pm | Posted - Feb 10th, 2019 @ 7:51am

13

PROVO - Multiple reports have been filed in recent weeks of children's toys being abandoned on the doorsteps of college girls close to BYU Campus. The most common toys being left are old, strangely marked dolls.

The reports started filing in toward the end of January according to Officer Stuart of the Provo police department. "Multiple girls have expressed their fear for their safety. Ultimately, this is our responsibility to look out for them. Many of these girls are far from home and are already nervous. Pranks like this are not as funny as some think."

Though no violent acts have yet occured, police are getting worried and are taking increased security measures around the south of BYU campus.

Some girls have expressed more interest in this subject than others. Shelby, a student at BYU, is one of these. "Some of us live far away from home, and the feeling that someone is watching us, is unnerving and worries us."

The Presidient of Brigham Young University, Kevin P. Worthen expressed his interest for students safety. "Female students are encouraged to reach out to the police and school counselors if anything suspicious or potentially dangerous occurs." If Zan Ferrin is still reading this then we got you.
